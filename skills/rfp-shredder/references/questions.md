# Go/No-Go Qualification Questions

Standard qualification questions used by the RFP Shredder skill. Each category is weighted and each question scored 1-5.

Customize these questions, weights, and scoring guidance to match your organization.

---

## Decision Thresholds

| Weighted Score | Decision |
|---|---|
| 80%+ | **Strong Go** - Pursue with confidence |
| 65-79% | **Conditional Go** - Pursue, but address flagged risks before committing |
| 50-64% | **Executive Review Required** - Escalate; significant risks present |
| Below 50% | **No-Go** - Do not pursue unless strategic override is justified |

---

## Category 1: Strategic Alignment
**Weight: 20%**

| # | Question | Scoring Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| 1.1 | Does the issuing organization match our Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)? | 5 = Perfect ICP match (Tier 1 bank, global footprint). 3 = Adjacent fit (regional bank, credit union). 1 = Outside ICP entirely. |
| 1.2 | What is the strategic value of this client to our business? | 5 = Marquee logo, opens new market or vertical. 3 = Solid client but no strategic premium. 1 = Low-value, no strategic upside. |
| 1.3 | Is there real growth potential beyond this initial contract? | 5 = Clear multi-year expansion path, cross-sell opportunities. 3 = Possible but unconfirmed. 1 = One-off engagement, no follow-on. |
| 1.4 | Does this opportunity align with our current go-to-market priorities? | 5 = Core focus area. 3 = Adjacent to priorities. 1 = Completely outside current strategy. |

---

## Category 2: Relationship & RFP Origins
**Weight: 10%**

| # | Question | Scoring Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| 2.1 | How did we become aware of this RFP? (Warm intro, existing relationship, public notice, cold) | 5 = We helped shape the RFP or were invited directly. 3 = Warm introduction or known contact. 1 = Cold/public notice, no prior engagement. |
| 2.2 | What is the strength of our existing relationship with the buyer? | 5 = Trusted advisor status with decision-makers. 3 = Some contacts, limited depth. 1 = No relationship. |
| 2.3 | Do we have executive sponsorship or a senior champion inside the organization? | 5 = Active champion at decision-making level. 3 = Mid-level contact who is supportive. 1 = No internal advocate. |
| 2.4 | How much insight do we have into their decision process, evaluation criteria, and internal politics? | 5 = Deep understanding of process and key players. 3 = Some visibility. 1 = Completely opaque. |

---

## Category 3: Competitive Landscape
**Weight: 10%**

| # | Question | Scoring Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| 3.1 | Are we the incumbent, or are we trying to unseat an existing vendor? | 5 = We are the incumbent. 3 = No incumbent / greenfield. 1 = Strong incumbent in place. |
| 3.2 | Do we know who else is likely bidding? | 5 = Yes, and we have clear advantages over them. 3 = Some idea of competitors. 1 = No visibility into competitive field. |
| 3.3 | Can we clearly articulate why we are different and better for this specific opportunity? | 5 = Strong, defensible differentiators. 3 = Some differentiation but not unique. 1 = Commodity position, no clear edge. |

---

## Category 4: Resource Requirements
**Weight: 15%**

| # | Question | Scoring Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| 4.1 | Does our proposal team have bandwidth to deliver a quality response within the deadline? | 5 = Team has capacity and timeline is comfortable. 3 = Tight but manageable with prioritization. 1 = Team is overloaded; quality will suffer. |
| 4.2 | Is the submission deadline realistic given the complexity of the RFP? | 5 = Generous timeline. 3 = Achievable but requires focus. 1 = Unreasonably tight. |
| 4.3 | Can we reuse significant existing content, or does this require mostly bespoke drafting? | 5 = 70%+ reusable from content library. 3 = 40-60% reusable. 1 = Mostly bespoke, heavy lift. |
| 4.4 | Are the required SMEs available to contribute within the response window? | 5 = All key SMEs available and committed. 3 = Most available with some gaps. 1 = Critical SMEs unavailable. |
| 4.5 | Can our delivery/implementation team realistically fulfill this if we win? | 5 = Standard deployment, proven playbook. 3 = Feasible with some adaptation. 1 = Would require significant new capability or hiring. |

---

## Category 5: Commercial Viability
**Weight: 15%**

| # | Question | Scoring Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| 5.1 | Is there a clear, realistic budget stated or implied? | 5 = Budget stated and aligns with our pricing. 3 = Budget vague but likely in range. 1 = No budget visibility, or clearly below our floor. |
| 5.2 | Is the contract term long enough to justify the sales and onboarding cost? | 5 = Multi-year contract (3+ years). 3 = 1-2 year term. 1 = Short-term pilot or sub-12 months with no commitment. |
| 5.3 | Are the pricing and commercial terms flexible enough for a viable deal? | 5 = Standard commercial terms, room for negotiation. 3 = Some rigid terms but workable. 1 = Highly restrictive, penalties, or unfavorable payment terms. |
| 5.4 | What is the estimated Annual Contract Value (ACV)? Does it meet our minimum deal size? | 5 = Significantly above minimum. 3 = At or near minimum. 1 = Below minimum deal threshold. |

## Category 6: Requirement Fit
**Weight: 20%**

| # | Question | Scoring Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| 6.1 | How closely do our core platform capabilities match the stated technical requirements? | 5 = Near-complete coverage out of the box. 3 = Covers majority, gaps in non-critical areas. 1 = Major gaps in core requirements. |
| 6.2 | What is their preference for hosting/deployment model? (On-prem, private cloud, SaaS/public cloud, hybrid) | 5 = SaaS/public cloud preferred or accepted. 3 = Flexible, will consider SaaS alongside other options. 1 = On-prem or private cloud mandatory; SaaS excluded. **NOTE: This is a potential deal-breaker. If on-prem is mandatory, flag as NON-COMPLIANT.** |
| 6.3 | Can we meet the integration requirements with existing connectors and APIs? | 5 = All integrations available or straightforward. 3 = Most available, 1-2 require custom work. 1 = Multiple complex integrations not currently supported. |
| 6.4 | What level of customization is required beyond standard configuration? | 5 = Standard config covers requirements. 3 = Moderate customization needed. 1 = Heavy custom development required. |
| 6.5 | Can we meet the stated implementation timeline? | 5 = Comfortably within timeline. 3 = Achievable but tight. 1 = Timeline unrealistic for scope. |
| 6.6 | Do we have relevant case studies or references in this sector? | 5 = Multiple strong references in same sector and similar scale. 3 = Some relevant references, not exact match. 1 = No references in sector. |

---

## Category 7: Legal, Security & Compliance
**Weight: 10%**

| # | Question | Scoring Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| 7.1 | Do we hold the mandatory certifications required? (e.g., SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, PCI DSS) | 5 = All certifications held and current. 3 = Most held, 1 in progress. 1 = Missing critical certifications with no path to obtain. |
| 7.2 | Can we meet the data residency and sovereignty requirements? | 5 = Data residency fully supported in required regions. 3 = Supported with minor changes. 1 = Cannot meet residency requirements. |
| 7.3 | Are the contract terms (liability, indemnity, SLAs, penalties) within acceptable range? | 5 = Standard terms, minor negotiation expected. 3 = Some aggressive terms but negotiable. 1 = Onerous terms that legal would likely reject. |
| 7.4 | Do we meet the regulatory and compliance framework requirements for this jurisdiction? | 5 = Fully compliant. 3 = Mostly compliant, minor gaps addressable. 1 = Significant compliance gaps. |

---

## Customization Notes

- Add, remove, or reword questions to match your industry and risk appetite.
- Adjust weights to reflect organizational priorities. Heavily regulated industries may weight Legal & Compliance higher.
- Some questions require internal knowledge the RFP will not answer (team bandwidth, relationship strength). The skill flags these as "REQUIRES USER INPUT."
- Question 6.2 is flagged as a deal-breaker because the example company in capabilities.md is SaaS-only. Adjust if your deployment model differs.
